# Machine Learning for Healthcare (Spring 2019)

## I. Course Introduction & Overview

*   **Course Title:** Machine Learning for Healthcare (Spring 2019)
*   **Instructors:** David Sontag (Professor, Computer Science; Institute for Medical Engineering and Science), Pete Solvitz (Co-instructor).
*   **Course Focus:** To explore how machine learning (ML) and artificial intelligence (AI) can be used as one piece of a larger puzzle to transform healthcare.

### A. The Problem: Challenges in US Healthcare

*   **High Costs:** Healthcare in the United States costs too much, currently at $3 trillion per year.
*   **Suboptimal Quality of Care:**
    *   **Chronic Diseases:** Often diagnosed late and not managed well.
    *   **Medical Errors:** Happen frequently, leading to needless deaths and worsening of disease.
*   **Personal Impact:** Healthcare challenges impact everyone, affecting quality of life, work, studies, and sometimes leading to needless deaths.
*   **Limitations of ML/AI:** ML/AI will only be one piece of the puzzle; many other systematic changes are required in the healthcare system.

### B. Examples of Healthcare Challenges (Personal Stories)

*   **Late Diagnosis (Alzheimer's Disease):** The instructor's grandfather was diagnosed quite late, impacting the family's ability to recognize disease-related behaviors.
*   **Suboptimal Management and Treatment Initiation (Multiple Myeloma):** The instructor's mother died without initiating treatment for Multiple Myeloma (a blood cancer), despite being in early stages and having over 20 available treatments. Her death was due to light chains accumulating in her heart, highlighting a lack of recognition of the disease's progression.

### C. Today's Lecture Agenda

1.  Background on Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning in healthcare.
2.  Discussion on why 2019 is the opportune time to address this problem.
3.  Examples of how machine learning is likely to transform healthcare over the next decade, guided by current research.
4.  Overview of what is unique and different about applying machine learning in the healthcare domain compared to other fields.

## II. Historical Context: Early AI in Medicine (1960s-1980s)

*   **Early Efforts:** Artificial intelligence in medicine is not a new field, with origins dating back to the 1960s or 1970s. Despite effective algorithms, they often did not translate into widespread clinical care.

### A. MYCIN System (Stanford, 1960s/70s)

*   **Definition:** One of the earliest examples of using AI for diagnosis.
*   **Goal:** To identify bacteria causing infection and guide appropriate therapy.
